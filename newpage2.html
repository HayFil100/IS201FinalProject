Outer Space

"I love learning about our solar system — it inspires me!"

Outer space is an enormous, mysterious expanse that begins just beyond Earth’s atmosphere and stretches farther than we can fully comprehend. It contains everything—stars, planets, galaxies, black holes, and vast regions of near-empty vacuum. Although it may seem quiet and still, space is constantly in motion. Stars are born, galaxies collide, and planets orbit their suns in an intricate cosmic dance.

Scientists estimate that most of the universe is made up of dark matter and dark energy—mysterious substances we cannot see directly, yet whose effects shape the expansion and gravity of the universe. Telescopes like the James Webb Space Telescope allow us to observe galaxies formed shortly after the Big Bang.

  • Space is huge
    1. The universe is still expanding
    2. There is no known edge or boundary
    3. Its true size remains unknown
  • Space is extremely cold
    1. The average temperature is −455°F
    2. Heat escapes quickly without air
    3. Only stars and planets provide warmth
  • Space is mostly empty
    1. Most of space is a vacuum
    2. Atoms are spread incredibly far apart
    3. You could travel vast distances without collision

Space inspires exploration and imagination. Humans have walked on the Moon, lived aboard the International Space Station, and sent robotic explorers to Mars in search of signs of past life. Space exploration has also helped us better understand Earth and its climate.
